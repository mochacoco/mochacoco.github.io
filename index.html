Profile

I am a Ph.D. candidate in ME at Seoul National University, advised by Prof. Yong-Lae Park in the Soft Robotics and Bionics Lab (SRBL). My research interests include control systems of soft robots, machine intelligence, and nonlinear system identifications. I am currently working on developing learning and adaptive systems for soft robot manipulators. In the past, I have worked on probabilistic machine learning techniques for soft robot proprioceptions.

Selected Publications

Adaptive Calibration of Soft Sensors Using Optimal Transportation Transfer Learning for Mass Production and Long-Term Usage

[PDF] [Website]

by DongWook Kim, Junghan Kwon, Byungjun Jeon, and Yong-Lae Park

published in Advanced Intelligent Systems, 2020.

Soft sensors suffer from high manufacturing tolerances and signal drift from long‐term usage, which degrades their practicality. We developed an adaptive calibration method for soft sensors, suitable for mass production and long‐term usage.
